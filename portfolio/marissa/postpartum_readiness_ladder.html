Postpartum movement readiness: a step-by-step guide to returning to exercise

Check off what you can do. Progress when a stage is complete.
Ready: 0 of 4 stages
1
Foundation
Weeks 1 – 6
▼
Start here
  • Diaphragmatic breathing feels natural
  • Can gently contract and fully release pelvic floor
  • Short walks (10–15 min) without symptoms
  • No heaviness or leaking during daily activities
2
Strength base
Weeks 6 – 12
▼
Build slowly
  • 30 min brisk walk without symptoms
  • 10 single-leg calf raises each side, no pressure
  • Bodyweight squats with good control
  • Stairs feel effortless and symptom-free
3
Impact readiness
Weeks 12 – 16+
▼
Check carefully
  • Single-leg squat with stable pelvis, no lean
  • Jog in place for 1 min — no leak or heaviness
  • 10 single-leg hops each side, no symptoms
  • Core feels connected, no doming or coning
4
Return to running
16+ weeks (when stages 1–3 complete)
▼
You're ready
  • Start with run/walk intervals (1 min run, 1 min walk)
  • No symptoms during or after each session
  • Gradually increase run intervals over 4–6 weeks
  • Pain, leaking, or heaviness = back to previous stage
